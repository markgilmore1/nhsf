§ After years of negotiation would-be tourist hotel developer Mawland Hotel Management has only until 30 June 2006 to satisfy the State Government that it has met all the conditions necessary to secure a lease over that site - another integral part of a North Head sanctuary. What can you do?

§ The Australian Institute of Police Management is planning a substantial redevelopment and some changes to the use of its Collins Beach site. These changes will also increase impacts on North Head and its values. What can you do?

If the sanctuary is to become a reality, now is the time to act.

The issues need publicity - political decisions-makers are influenced by public opinion. Please express your views now.

The North Head Sanctuary Foundation needs your help. Ask your friends to join the campaign too.

What you can do

1. Write to NSW Premier The Hon Morris Iemma
at Parliament House, Macquarie St Sydney NSW 2000.
- Urge him to get behind an ecological sanctuary across North Head.
- Ask him to talk with the Commonwealth to break the impasse over the leases needed to establish the core of a sanctuary on the School of Artillery site.
- Ask him to withdraw from the Conditional Agreement to lease the historic Quarantine Station to Mawland because after years of negotiation, they have still not met all the conditions required to protect the area.

2. Write to Federal Environment Minister, The Hon. Ian Campbell,
Minister for the Environment & Heritage, at Parliament House Canberra ACT 2600
- Congratulate him on the recent National Heritage listing for North Head.
- Urge him to ensure that no works proposed at the Quarantine Station, the Australian Institute of Police Management, or elsewhere on North Head are allowed to proceed until he is fully satisfied that they will not impact adversely on the National Heritage values of this outstanding area.

3. Call talk-back radio and write letters to newspapers highlighting the above issues.
